USA - Route 66

Welcome to my favorite trip so far. It is big and exauting but it's completely worth it. ItÂ´s the kind of experience that everyone should go through, it changed my way to face my life and the world. I did it with my family (me, my brother and my parents), I should say that 4 people is the perfect number to do this kind of trip. It is perfect to fit in the car and you can share a room for 4 people that is much cheaper than if you go alone.

After a long research, I realised that most sites suggested to do it in two or three weeks. But we wanted to enjoy the most of it so we did it in 25 days (from 1st August to 25th August of 2017). And if you are thinking that it is too expensive because it is almost 5000 kilometers and 24 nights, I should say that the prices are nothing like in Europe. The gas is much cheaper, you can fill the car's tank with 20 euros and we paid in average 100 euros per night for the four of us (staying in city centers). Let's be honest, in Europe this would be impossible.

The Route 66 is an historic road of the USA, known as "The Mother Road" and it goes from Chicago to Los Angeles. Along the way we did a little detour to visit the Grand Canyon and Las Vegas (these places don't belong to the Route 66 but they were a really good upgrade to the trip). So in total we visited the following cities: Chicago IL, Pontiac IL, Springfield IL, St. Louis MO, Springfield MO, Tulsa OK, Oklahoma City OK, Amarillo TX, Midpoint TX, Tucumcari NM, Santa Rosa NM, Santa Fe NM, Albuquerque NM, Gallup NM, Flagstaff AZ, Grand Canyon AZ, Kingman AZ, Las Vegas NV, Needles CA and Los Angeles CA.

There are a good variety of restaurant chains along the road, but a think that if you find these ones, you should really try them: Applebee's and Shake Shack.

Chicago

First stop: my favorite city! We stayed 4 days in Chicago and I think it was enough to see the most important monuments and to know a completely different city than we are used to. There everything is huge, this is the perfect word to describe this city. It made us fell in love with it instantely. It could also be because we see a lot of TV series about Chicago, nevertheless we were amazed.

Little advice: if you are not really interested in paying to see stuff, I should advise you to go up a skyscraper. My favorite one was Skydeck Chicago, because you have like a small window where the floor is made of glass and you can see the whole city and it is spectacular!

- Millennium Park
- Navy Pier
- Skydeck Chicago
- John Hancock Tower (360 Chicago)
- Beaches
- Magnificent Mile Shopping
- The beginning of Route 66 (Corner of Michigan Avenue and Adams Street)

Pontiac

Quick stop in Pontiac to get the kick off of Route 66. This is an important stop to enter the spirit of the Mother Road.

- Route 66 Association
- Hall of Fame & Museum
- Murals
- Downtown

Springfield IL

After 330 kilometers we arrived at Springfield, Illinois. A city much smaller and quieter than Chicago but full of history. Balen's is a really nice sports bar with very friendly people were I tryed the most spicy burger ever.

- Lincoln Tomb & War Memorials
- Illinois State Capitol
- Lincoln Memorial Garden
- Washington Park

St. Louis

Next morning, after 157 kilometers, we arrived at a new state: Missouri, city of St. Louis. Bigger than Springfield and with a lot more pleople and traffic.

- Gateway Arch
- Forest Park St Louis
- Busch Stadium
- St. Louis Cathedral
- City Garden St Louis
- Soulard

Springfield MO

It's time for a new Springfield, this time in Missouri, 350 kilometers from St. Louis.

Tulsa

Welcome to the state of Oklahoma, 290 kilometers from Springfield comes the city of Tulsa. The day before we arrived here there was a tornado so the city was a bit destroyed but it was a new and dramatic experience for sure.

- The Cave House
- Center of the Universe
- Holy Family Cathedral
- The Golden Driller

Oklahoma City

170 kilometers later, we arrived at Oklahoma City. The biggest city (after Chicago of course) until know.

- Myriad Botanical Gardens
- Chesapeake Energy Arena
- Chickasaw National Recreation Area
- Beavers Bend
- Turner Falls
- Heavener Runestone State Park

Amarillo

Welcome to the state of Texas, 416 kilometers from Oklahoma we have Amarillo. What a great reception we had! Very friendly people :) And a really nice restaurant: Red River Steakhouse.

- Jack Sisemore Traveland RV Museum
- Cadillac Ranch
- Amarillo Botanical Gardens
- Route 66 Historic District
- Bill's Backyard Classics
- Second Amendment Cowboy

Midpoint

Here we are, in the middle of our journey: Midpoint, TX. You have to try a pie in the famous and historic Midpoint Cafe.

Tucumcari

New Mexico is the new state, city of Tucumcari (180 kilometers from Amarillo). A place that takes us back in time to cowboy's movies.

- Murals of Tucumcari
- Tee Pee Curios
- Route 66 Townhouse Welcome Center & Gift Shop
- New Mexico Route 66 Museum
- Tucumcari Trading Post
- Route 66 Monument

Santa Rosa

A really quick stop to see this beautiful blue lake called Blue Hole, nobody was able to get to the bottom yet!

Santa Fe

Just a quick stop to see this beautiful town that feels like we went back in time. And a nice restaurant to eat a burrito: The Burrito Company.

Albuquerque

Still in New Mexico, after 280 kilometers we arrived at Albuquerque. At that time there was construction everyone we had the impression that they were renewing the city. This city has two distinct parts: the Old Town and the new one.

- Albuquerque Biological Park
- Old Town Albuquerque
- Unser Racing Museum
- Central Avenue
- Old Town Antiques

Gallup

Two and a half hours on the road and we arrived at Gallup. Still in New Mexico we face ourselves with a flashback, a exposition of old cars (just like the ones we seen in movies) in the middle of town.

- Bill Malone Trading Company
- Gallup Flea Market
- Rex Museum
- Manuelito Visitor Center

Flagstaff

Almost 400 kilometers later, we arrived at the state of Arizona, city of Flagstaff. I should say that each state seems from a different country, the landscape is so different. And here, in Arizona, it is just breathtaking. It is so beautiful that you want to take pictures of everything but the camera can't capture the beauty that you see with your eyes. A really nice restaurant: Beaver Street Brewery.

- Buffalo Park
- Red Mountain Trail
- Grand Falls of the Little Colorado River
- Lake Mary
- Historic Downtown and Railroad District
- Lava River Cave
- Wet Beaver Creek

Grand Canyon

Next stop: favorite experience! A little detour from the Route 66, 125 kilometers from Flagstaff comes what I call the only place that left me amazed every day I was there. I couldn't get enough of that view. And it is like I said before, we want to take pictures to remember it later but the camera can't capture so much beauty. I think I can describe it as monumental, grandiose, majestic and so much more that I have no words for it. You should see for yourself.

We stayed here 3 days and if we had the time I would have stayed for a week or so. But as we chose to stay in the Lodges of the Grand Canyon National Park it would be like a small fortune to stay there a week. Although I think it is the best way to get the experience, one day we wake up and we had a deer at our door. It was priceless!

Big advice: this accommodation has to be one of the first to book. There aren't many lodges in the park compared with the people that want to go there, so they get full really fast.

Kingman

Back to the Mother Road, 280 kilometers later, we arrived at Kingman. It was a quite nice surprise in the middle of nowhere! We were welcomed with a BBQ at the hotel.

- Arizona Route 66 Museum
- Cool Springs Station

Las Vegas

Just 165 kilometers away from Kingman is the Sin City: Las Vegas. The city where you can travel across the world just in one street, the Stip. If you have no idea of what to expect in Las Vegas, be prepared because this city will surprise you every moment of every day. We like to say that Las Vegas is fake but unique! Just a little tip: have you ever imagine a storm inside a shopping mall which is inside a hotel? Well, it happened! And I won't spoil it anymore, you should see for yourself. We stayed here 3 days and I think it was enough to get the idea of the spirit.

- Bellagio Fountains
- The Strip
- Hoover Dam Bypass
- Stratosphere Tower
- Mirage Volcano
- Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas Sign
- Las Vegas Downtown
- Fashion Show Mall
- Town Square Las Vegas
- Miracle Mile Shops at Planet Hollywood

Needles

180 kilometers away from the Sin City is Neddles, already in California. I should admit that this city is the one that you just stop to rest and move to the next one. It doesn't have nothing interesting to see, the only thing that I can point out is the Pirate Cove Resort. It is kind of interesting to visit because you can see a different way to be at beaches. Instead of having a towel to lay down, they have a boat.

Los Angeles

Final stop: Los Angeles! 415 kilometers from Needles. A really big city and, for us, a bit disappointing. We were shocked by the number of homeless people that we found. I understand that it is the city of dreams, that everyone goes to be famous and sometimes that goes bad, but I wasn't expecting that. Besides this, this is a city worth visiting, because you have it all. You have beaches, you have downtown, you have parks, you have Beverly Hills and, of course, you have Hollywood.

We stayed in Los Angeles for five days. When we arrived we were really tired so we didn't have so much energy as at the beginning and the traffic also didn't help (be prepared to spend a few hours in traffic). So our sightseeing was a bit slower. Thus I kept the impression that we didn't see all, I think we saw the most important for us.
